Work and Efficiency

Pick one task and then do it.

This is very difficult because sometimes our projects aren’t a 30-minute or one-hour job. It may take 8 hours or multiple days. What’s the answer then? Break it up into one task and then do that one task to its completion. But how long should that be? And what’s reasonable? I think most of our days are broken into hour segments. Which then is really 45 to 50 minutes. We have meetings that start on the hour, take a lunch hour, or schedule a call for an hour. Working consistently for 45 minutes on one thing makes sense, but is very difficult. The Pomodoro technique suggests 25-minute blocks of time, with short 5 minute breaks, followed by longer breaks later on. Pomodoro helps you eliminate interruptions and helps you estimate how long a certain task will take you. Read more about the Pomodoro technique in this article.
